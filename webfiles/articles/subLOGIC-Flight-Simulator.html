Following its release as the subLOGIC Flight Simulator, this program pushed the graphics capabilities of early personal computers to the limit. The subLOGIC Flight Simulator was later licensed to Microsoft for sales on the IBM PC and the Macintosh and is the ancestor of the current flight simulator products from Microsoft. |!4 Early History of the PC Flight Simulator[1][2] | |! |!
